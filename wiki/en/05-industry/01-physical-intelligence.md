# Physical Intelligence

> Robotics foundation models / general-purpose physical AI software

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2024 (stealth emerged March 2024; seed announced around the same time)
- **Founding team (representative):** Karol Hausman (CEO), Sergey Levine, Lachy Groom, Brian Ichter, Chelsea Finn (plus other key leaders such as Adnan Esmail, Quan Vuong)
- **Total funding (publicly reported):** about **\$470M** as of the late-2024 round (seed **\$70M** + reported **\$400M** round)

---

## Company direction & strengths (model vs hardware)

Physical Intelligence is primarily **model-centric**: it focuses on building **foundation AI models for robots** that generalize across tasks and physical settings (rather than selling a single-purpose robot arm).

Its flagship theme is: **learn the “chaos” of the physical world** with generalizable foundation model principles, then apply them across different embodied forms and tasks.

Notable positioning:

- **Generalist physical AI software**: invest once in base/“foundation” capabilities, then adapt/fine-tune for downstream physical applications.
- **Full-stack ambition**: model architecture + training recipes + data strategy + deployment reliability (recovery, robustness).

---

## Config vs Physical Intelligence

### Main differences

- **Emphasis:** both pursue general-purpose robotics intelligence, but Physical Intelligence is more explicitly **a robotics foundation model company** (model-first across embodiments).
- **Route to product:** driven by foundation model training/adaptation; hardware is not the core deliverable.

### Potential overlap / competition

- Where Physical Intelligence makes perception/action representations that are “robot-ready,” Config’s differentiation on the **data-to-action-label interface** (precision/accuracy + scenario-level coverage + quality gates) becomes a key contrast.

---

## Config's differentiation (why Config is different)

Config’s core edge is the **bimanual product pipeline**:

- **Human-operated hand gripper** to make action labels measurable and robot-aligned.
- **Scenario-based diversity planning** (coverage management via clustering like UMAP + HDBSCAN).
- **Closed-loop operations** so improvements accumulate quickly in the physical world (human recovery + labeling/quality refinement).

Even when other companies compete on model scale, Config differentiates by making the “data usability → deployability” link predictable.
