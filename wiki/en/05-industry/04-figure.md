# Figure

> General-purpose humanoid robots (robot hardware + embedded AI)

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2022
- **Founder:** Brett Adcock
- **Total funding (publicly reported):** roughly **\$1.75B+** cumulative (Series A \$70M + Series B \$675M + Series C \$1B+)

---

## Company direction & strengths (model vs hardware)

Figure is primarily a **hardware company** focused on deploying **autonomous general-purpose humanoid robots** for real-world labor (factories, logistics, and eventually homes).

Key characteristics:

- builds humanoid robots (mechanics + sensors + compute) and ships iterative product generations
- integrates AI/VLA-style perception/action components so robots can process natural language and learn from experience

Strength themes:

- end-to-end robot system engineering (manufacturing, integration, safety)
- roadmap toward higher autonomy through model integration and real-world practice

---

## Config vs Figure

### Main differences

- **Emphasis:** Figure delivers the robot product itself; Config focuses on the **data + model stack** for bimanual general-purpose tasks.
- **Product boundary:** Figure optimizes the full “robot + embedded stack” boundary, while Config optimizes **action representation usability** and **closed-loop improvements**.

### Config's differentiation

If Config integrates with humanoid ecosystems, its differentiator is making perception/action interfaces and labeling reliable:

- human gripper → robot-aligned action representation
- scenario coverage planning + quality gates
- human-in-the-loop online iteration

This reduces the integration cost when deploying any frontier model onto real workcells.
