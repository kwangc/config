# Boston Dynamics

> Advanced robotics company focused on mobile manipulation and locomotion

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2004 (spun out from MIT)
- **Key team:** Marc Raibert (Founder/Chairman), Aaron Saunders (Interim CEO)
- **Ownership:** Hyundai Motor Group (acquired majority stake 2020; subsequent funding)
- **HQ:** Waltham, Massachusetts
- **Flagship products:** Atlas (humanoid), Stretch (mobile manipulator)

---

## Company direction & strengths

Boston Dynamics is **hardware research-first**, focused on advancing the state of robot locomotion and mobility. Recent pivot toward commercial applications after years of R&D.

Key strengths:
- **Locomotion expertise:** Unmatched reputation for dynamic motion (running, jumping, balancing on Atlas); decades of MIT origins
- **Research to product transition:** Stretch (mobile manipulator for warehouses) represents first major commercial push
- **Mobility + manipulation combo:** Unlike pure arm companies, BD integrates locomotion with manipulation
- **Hyundai backing:** Access to manufacturing scale and automotive domain expertise

## Config vs Boston Dynamics

### Main differences

- **Research vs product:** BD is transitioning from research to commercialization; Config is focused on production data and deployable policies
- **Humanoid vs targeted platforms:** Atlas is general-purpose humanoid research; Config ships bimanual systems in specific domains (kitchens, factories)
- **Software vs hardware:** BD's moat is locomotion hardware; Config's moat is data / policy software

### Potential overlap

- **Dexterous tasks on mobile platforms:** Stretch (mobile manipulator) could eventually benefit from Config-like data-centric dexterity models
- **Task generalization:** Both pursue multi-task policy learning, but at different scales (BD: locomotion + gross manipulation, Config: fine-grained bimanual)

### Config's differentiation

- **Narrow specialization:** Config owns bimanual dexterous tasks; BD is broader (whole-body, general-purpose)
- **Data & scenario focus:** Config's differentiator is systematic scenario mining and quality gates; BD's is raw locomotion/mechanics capability
- **Deployment velocity:** Config can iterate faster on specific domains; BD has longer research cycles but broader scope
