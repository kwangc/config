# Apptronik

> Humanoid robot company focused on workforce augmentation

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2016
- **Key team:** Jeff Cardenas (CEO)
- **Funding:** ~$20M+ Series A/funding rounds
- **HQ:** Austin, Texas
- **Flagship:** Apollo — full-body humanoid robot for industrial tasks

---

## Company direction & strengths

Apptronik is **hardware-focused**, building full-body humanoid robots (Apollo) designed for workforce augmentation in manufacturing, logistics, and industrial settings.

Key strengths:
- **Full-body design:** Bipedal + two-arm system built for general industrial tasks
- **Teleoperation-first:** Emphasizes human-in-the-loop remote operation + autonomous learning from demos
- **Safety focus:** Designed for safe human-robot collaboration in industrial environments
- **Early deployments:** Partnered with manufacturing companies for pilot programs

---

## Config vs Apptronik

### Main differences

- **Form factor:** Apptronik builds full-body humanoids; Config is bimanual arms
- **Control paradigm:** Apptronik emphasizes teleoperation + remote human control; Config is autonomous policy-driven
- **Scale:** Apptronik is larger, full-body; Config is precise, tabletop-scale

### Potential overlap

- **Dexterous task learning:** Apollo will need arm-level dexterity; could benefit from Config-style data pipelines
- **Teleoperation as data source:** Both pursue learning from human demonstrations

### Config's differentiation

- **Autonomy focus:** Config builds autonomous policies; Apptronik emphasizes human teleoperation
- **Scale of operation:** Config targets high-precision tasks on tabletops; Apptronik targets full-body workforce augmentation
- **Data strategy:** Config invests in systematic scenario mining; Apptronik focuses on teleoperated trajectories
