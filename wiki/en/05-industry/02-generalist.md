# Generalist

> Embodied foundation models for dexterity / general-purpose robots

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2024
- **Founding team (representative):** Andy Zeng, Pete Florence, Andrew Barry
- **Total funding (publicly reported):** seed round exists (reported March 2025) but the **exact amount is undisclosed** in available public summaries

---

## Company direction & strengths (model vs hardware)

Generalist is primarily a **model-first / embodied AI** company.

Core direction:

- build **embodied foundation models** that can scale with high-fidelity physical interaction
- start with **dexterity** as the focus area, then expand toward broader general-purpose robotics

What they emphasize as strengths:

- large-scale AI + robotics as core DNA
- scaling laws/data/model/hardware co-design for robust manipulation
- early public demos that highlight learned sensorimotor policies in unstructured settings

---

## Config vs Generalist

### Main differences

- **Emphasis:** both companies pursue “general-purpose” robotics, but Generalist is positioned as an **embodied foundation model company** focused on dexterity and interaction scaling.
- **Product path:** Generalist pushes toward dexterous embodied models; Config pushes toward an end-to-end **data-to-deploy** pipeline for bimanual tasks and fast adaptation.

### Potential overlap / competition

- If Generalist’s embodied models become strong action policies that transfer across embodiments, Config competes by making **robot-aligned action interfaces** and **closed-loop ops** the reliable integration layer.

---

## Config's differentiation (why Config is different)

Config’s differentiators:

- **Action-label precision/accuracy** via a **human-operated hand gripper** that matches the target robot end-effector.
- **Scenario-level diversity & coverage management** (clustering + guided collection to reduce wasted variance).
- **Human-in-the-loop online improvement** that converges quickly in the physical world (fine-tune in ~24h, improve/deploy in ~48h).

This makes Config’s approach more directly deployable across bimanual workcells even when model backbones evolve quickly.
