# Unitree Robotics

> Chinese robotics company specializing in legged robots and quadrupeds

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2016 (Hangzhou-based)
- **Key team:** Wang Shensheng (Founder/CEO)
- **Funding:** Series A/B/C; privately held
- **HQ:** Hangzhou, China
- **Flagship products:** Unitree Go 1/Go 2 (quadrupedal robots), H1 (bipedal)

---

## Company direction & strengths

Unitree is **hardware-focused**, manufacturing affordable legged robots (quadrupeds and bipeds) for research, inspection, and emerging industrial applications.

Key strengths:
- **Cost-to-capability ratio:** Unitree Go 2 is ~$16k; vastly cheaper than Boston Dynamics/Agility equivalent hardware
- **Rapid iteration:** Fast hardware release cycle (Go 1 → Go 2); accessible to researchers
- **Quadruped specialization:** Deep expertise in gait control for four-legged platforms
- **Global reach:** Sold to thousands of customers across academia and industry

---

## Config vs Unitree Robotics

### Main differences

- **Platform type:** Unitree is legged (quads/bipeds); Config is stationary bimanual arms
- **Cost philosophy:** Unitree prioritizes accessibility and cost; Config prioritizes dexterity and precision
- **Task domain:** Unitree = locomotion + gross manipulation; Config = fine-grained bimanual precision

### Potential overlap

- **Affordability at scale:** If Unitree adds arm modules to Go 2/H1, could compete in bimanual robotics
- **Research collaborations:** Academic overlap in learning-from-demonstration and policy learning

### Config's differentiation

- **Dexterous manipulation:** Unitree's legged robots are not designed for fine-grained bimanual tasks (assembly, insertion, folding)
- **Data-centric approach:** Config's advantage is systematic data collection and scenario mining; Unitree's is platform affordability
- **Domain specialization:** Config owns the dexterous bimanual niche; Unitree owns the low-cost legged platform niche
