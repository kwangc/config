# Agility Robotics

> Legged humanoid robot for warehouse automation and logistics

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2015
- **Key team:** Damion Shelton (CEO), Jonathan Hurst (CTO, legged locomotion expert)
- **Funding:** ~$150M+ Series A/B/C (2024)
- **HQ:** Portland, Oregon
- **Flagship:** Digit — bipedal humanoid robot designed for logistics and parcel handling

---

## Company direction & strengths

Agility is **hardware-first**, building legged humanoid robots optimized for warehouse environments. Digit's bipedal design enables navigation through human-scale spaces (stairs, rough terrain, cluttered aisles) without environmental modification.

Key strengths:
- **Legged mobility:** Only major robot company shipping bipedal robots at scale; climbs stairs and crosses terrain where wheeled robots fail
- **Human-scale form factor:** 5'10" height, human-like reach — fits existing warehouse layouts
- **Real-world deployments:** Already operational at GXO, DHL warehouses for parcel handling
- **Specialization in locomotion:** Deep expertise in gait control, balance, terrain adaptation

## Config vs Agility Robotics

### Main differences

- **Hardware vs software:** Agility manufactures robots; Config builds data pipeline + policy software
- **Scale:** Digit is large, full-body; Config targets bimanual arms on tabletops/workbenches
- **Task domain:** Agility = mobile logistics (moving items across warehouses); Config = dexterous bimanual precision (insertion, assembly, folding)

### Potential overlap

- **Dexterous manipulation on mobile robots:** Digit will eventually need arm-level dexterity; Config's bimanual skills could be deployed on top of Digit's mobility platform
- **Data & learning:** As Agility scales to thousands of Digit robots, need for large-scale behavior cloning / policy learning will grow — could become a Config customer

### Config's differentiation

- **Bimanual task specialization:** Config's two-arm dexterous manipulation (gripper control, coordinated reach) is orthogonal to Digit's locomotion focus
- **Data-centric design:** Config's advantage is the data flywheel (scenario mining, quality gates, failure attribution); Agility's is the hardware/locomotion engineering
- **Task complexity tier:** Tasks Config targets (assembly, insertion) require fine-grained skill that full-body robots haven't specialized in yet
