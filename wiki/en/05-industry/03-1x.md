# 1x (1X Technologies)

> General-purpose humanoid robots for home/real environments (robot hardware + embedded AI/control)

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2014 (as Halodi Robotics; rebranded to 1X Technologies in 2022)
- **Founding team (representative):** Bernt Øivind Børnich (founder/CEO)
- **Total funding (publicly reported):** about **\$125M–\$126M** across rounds (public summaries vary by source)

---

## Company direction & strengths (model vs hardware)

1X is primarily a **robot hardware** company: it builds and deploys humanoid robots (e.g., EVE for industrial/logistics contexts, and NEO for home environments).

Direction:

- develop safe actuators and full-body control systems (historical focus)
- ship humanoids that can operate in more unstructured environments (domestic/home)

Strength themes:

- control + actuation engineering for whole-body motion
- iterative productization loops (prototype → updated models → expanded deployments)
- human/teleoperation support during early autonomy development

---

## Config vs 1x

### Main differences

- **Scope:** 1X is mostly about building and improving **the robot itself** (hardware-first).
- **Config focus:** Config is a **data + model product stack** for general-purpose bimanual tasks (software/data platform, not full robot manufacturing).

### Config’s distinction

Config can complement robot hardware companies by supplying:

- a robot-aligned action interface (data-label precision and measurable action representations)
- closed-loop operations that improve quickly on real workcells

In other words, Config competes at the “system integration + data usability” layer rather than manufacturing end-to-end humanoids.
