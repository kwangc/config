# Machina Labs

> Robotics company focused on humanoid robots for manufacturing

---

## Quick facts

- **Founded:** 2020
- **Key team:** Vaibhav Chetan (CEO), Bertrand Meyer (Co-founder)
- **Funding:** Series A funding (2023-24)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, California
- **Flagship:** Eva — humanoid robot for manufacturing and assembly

---

## Company direction & strengths

Machina Labs is **hardware-focused**, building cost-effective humanoid robots (Eva) designed for precision manufacturing and assembly tasks.

Key strengths:
- **Assembly specialization:** Unlike general-purpose humanoids, Eva is tuned for high-precision manufacturing tasks
- **Cost focus:** Targeting lower cost-per-unit than premium humanoid competitors
- **Precision manipulation:** Emphasis on arm-level dexterity for manufacturing
- **Early stage:** Emerging player with focused product roadmap

---

## Config vs Machina Labs

### Main differences

- **Platform:** Machina builds full-body humanoids; Config builds bimanual systems
- **Manufacturing focus:** Both target assembly/precise tasks; Machina via full-body humanoids, Config via bimanual arms
- **Deployment model:** Machina sells hardware; Config is software/policy-focused

### Potential overlap

- **Assembly & manufacturing:** Direct overlap in assembly, insertion, precision tasks
- **Competition:** If Eva achieves strong bimanual dexterity, could be direct competitor for Config's market

### Config's differentiation

- **Data moat:** Config's competitive advantage is the data pipeline and scenario mining; Machina's is hardware design
- **Specialization:** Config specializes in bimanual arm-level dexterity; Machina is full-body humanoid
- **Deployment flexibility:** Config's policies can be deployed on any robot hardware (transfer learning); Machina is tied to Eva hardware
